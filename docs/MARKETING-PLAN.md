# HeartWise Marketing Plan
**Goal:** 50 paying subscribers within 90 days (April–June 2026)
**Revenue target:** $1,950–$3,000/mo MRR

---

## The Honest Assessment

- 13 signups, 0 paying customers
- Most current users are personal contacts
- No social media presence, no content footprint, no SEO
- The product is strong — the distribution is zero

**The bottleneck is not the product. It's awareness.**

## Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1–2) — FREE

### 1. Twitter/X — Your Primary Channel
Medical Twitter is where your exact audience lives. Residents, fellows, med students — they're all there complaining about the same problems your tools solve.

**Account:** @HeartWiseMentor (or @MouzamFaroqui if personal brand)
**Post frequency:** 1x/day, 5 days/week

**Content formula — "The thing nobody told you":**
- Each post addresses ONE career decision mistake you've seen
- End with a hook that links to HeartWise (not every post — maybe 2 of 5)
- Use your real voice, your real experience

**Example posts:**

> "I've reviewed hundreds of physician contracts. The #1 thing residents miss: your non-compete clause can lock you out of your own city for 2 years after leaving.
>
> Most physicians don't read it until it's too late."

> "A PGY-3 asked me to review their fellowship app last week. Step 2: 255. 4 pubs. Great letters.
>
> Match probability? Under 50%.
>
> Why? Wrong specialty for their profile. They'd never benchmarked against actual matched applicants.
>
> This is fixable — but only if you check early enough."

> "The financial difference between a $350K academic job with PSLF and a $550K private practice job is NOT $200K/year.
>
> After taxes, loan forgiveness, retirement matching, and malpractice — the academic job often wins by year 12.
>
> Most physicians never run the numbers."

### 2. Reddit — Targeted, Not Spammy
**Subreddits:** r/medicalschool, r/residency, r/medicine, r/whitecoatinvestor

**Strategy:** Don't post about HeartWise. Post genuinely helpful answers to career questions. Add your URL to your profile/flair. When someone asks a question your tool answers perfectly, mention it naturally:

> "I built a tool that does exactly this — [link]. But here's the short answer..."

**Do NOT:** Spam links. Reddit will destroy you.

### 3. LinkedIn — Long-Form Authority
**Post frequency:** 2x/week
**Format:** Short career strategy posts from your attending perspective

> "I matched interventional cardiology as a US IMG. Here are the 5 things I did that most applicants don't..."

These build credibility. Connect with program directors, residency coordinators, medical education faculty.

## Phase 2: Targeted Outreach (Week 2–4) — FREE

### 4. Medical Student Organizations
Reach out directly to leadership of:
- **AMSA** (American Medical Student Association) — chapters at every school
- **SNMA** (Student National Medical Association)
- **AMA Medical Student Section**
- **Specialty-specific interest groups** at med schools (cardiology, surgery, etc.)
- **IMG organizations** — ECFMG communities, IMG-specific Facebook groups

**Pitch:** "Free 48-hour access to structured career decision tools. No credit card. Built by an interventional cardiologist who matched as a US IMG."

**Offer:** Give student org leaders **extended trial access** (1 week instead of 48h) in exchange for sharing with their members.

### 5. Residency Program Coordinators
This is underutilized by everyone.

- Email 10–15 program coordinators at programs you know
- Offer HeartWise as a free resource for their trainees
- Frame it as: "structured career planning tool for your residents — fellowship readiness, financial planning, contract review"
- Program coordinators LOVE being able to point residents to resources

### 6. The IMG Community (Your Strongest Edge)
You are a US IMG who matched interventional cardiology. That's your superpower.

- **Facebook groups:** "IMGs in USA," "USMLE Step 1/2 Prep," "IMG Match 2027"
- **WhatsApp groups:** IMG match groups (ask contacts to share)
- **YouTube comments:** Comment helpfully on IMG-focused channels (Dr. Jubbal, Med School Insiders, etc.)

**Your pitch for IMGs:**
> "I matched interventional cardiology as a US IMG. I built HeartWise because nobody told me how to actually make career decisions — which specialty, when to apply, how to position myself. These tools are what I wish I had."

## Phase 3: Content Marketing (Week 3–6) — FREE/LOW COST

### 7. Free Content That Drives Signups
Create 3–5 downloadable guides (PDF) gated behind email signup:

1. **"The Fellowship Match Timeline: Month-by-Month from PGY-2 to Match Day"** — This is pure gold for residents. Your v6 tool does this, but a free PDF version gets the email.
2. **"5 Contract Red Flags Every New Attending Should Know"** — Attendings share this with each other. Links to your Contract Review Tool.
3. **"IMG Match Strategy: What Actually Moves the Needle"** — Targets your strongest audience.
4. **"The Real Cost of Medical School Debt: PSLF vs Refinancing Calculator"** — Financial content gets shared.
5. **"How to Choose a Specialty When You're Interested in Everything"** — Med student catnip.

**Distribution:** Post on Twitter, Reddit, LinkedIn. Email to student orgs.

### 8. One YouTube Video (High Impact)
Record a 10-minute screen share: "I built career tools for physicians — let me show you what they do"

- Walk through 2–3 tools with a real scenario
- Show the actual output, the attending voice, the strategic recommendations
- Post on YouTube, share across all channels
- This single video can drive more signups than weeks of text posts

## Phase 4: Paid Growth (Week 6+) — IF Phase 1–3 shows traction

### 9. Instagram/TikTok Ads — $500/month

- These are low-competition, high-intent keywords
- Send to specific tool landing pages (you can create these later)

## Conversion Optimization

### What to Track
- **Landing page → signup rate** (target: 15%+)
- **Signup → tool run rate** (target: 60%+)
- **Trial → paid conversion rate** (target: 10%+)

### Quick Wins to Improve Conversion
1. **Add testimonials** — Ask your attending friends who signed up (Hiten, Vineet, John) to give 1-sentence quotes
2. **Show tool output previews** on landing page — the carousel is good, make sure it's compelling
3. **"Used by physicians at..."** — Once you have users from specific hospitals, list them (anonymized): "Used by physicians at Cleveland Clinic, NYU, Mount Sinai"

## What NOT to Do

1. **Don't buy followers or fake engagement** — medical professionals will see through it instantly
2. **Don't spam Reddit** — one ban and that channel is dead forever
3. **Don't spend money on ads before organic traction** — if the message doesn't resonate for free, paying won't fix it
4. **Don't try to be everywhere** — Twitter + Reddit + LinkedIn is enough for now
5. **Don't hire a marketing agency yet** — your authentic physician voice IS the marketing

---

## The One Thing That Matters Most

**You are the marketing.** An interventional cardiologist who matched as a US IMG, built real tools for career decisions, and speaks from experience — that's not something a marketing agency can fake.

Post from your own voice. Share real stories. Be the attending who says the thing nobody else says out loud.

The tools sell themselves once people try them. Your job is to get them to try.
