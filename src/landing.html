Common Questions

Is this just another AI chatbot? +
No. HeartWise is a structured decision platform built by a practicing interventional cardiologist. Every tool uses validated frameworks, real benchmark data (NRMP, MGMA, AAMC), and specialty-specific scoring. The AI assists with analysis, but the frameworks, scoring models, and career strategy are physician-designed. This is not a chatbot giving generic advice — it is a decision engine built on how physicians actually make career decisions.
Who built this? +
Dr. Mouzam Faroqui — a practicing interventional cardiologist who went through the entire training pipeline: medical school, internal medicine residency, cardiology fellowship, and interventional cardiology fellowship. Every tool reflects real decisions he made, mentored others through, or wishes someone had given him structured guidance on. This is not a tech company guessing what physicians need.
What happens during the 48-hour trial? +
Full access to all 15 tools. No credit card required. You can run any analysis — match probability, contract review, financial planning, specialty fit — and see your diagnostic results immediately. The only thing gated during the trial is the action plan (the "what to do about it" section). Most users know within one tool run whether HeartWise is worth it.
Is my data private and secure? +
Yes. Your data is stored in a secure, encrypted database (Supabase with row-level security). We do not sell your data, share it with third parties, or use it for advertising. Your career information is used only to power your tools and track your progress. You can delete your account and all associated data at any time.
I'm an attending. Is this only for trainees? +
No. HeartWise serves physicians at every career stage. Attendings use the contract review tool (most popular for renegotiation), financial projection tools, the burnout vs. misfit diagnostic, and career transition planning. Many attendings discover they are in the wrong practice model — not the wrong specialty — and HeartWise helps them see that distinction clearly.
How is this different from a career advisor or mentor? +
A mentor gives you their opinion. HeartWise gives you data. The match calculator scores your competitiveness against real benchmarks. The contract tool finds specific red flags with dollar values. The financial planner models your actual numbers. You get structured, reproducible analysis you can run again as your situation changes — not a single conversation that fades from memory. HeartWise is the tool you use between mentor meetings.
Can I cancel anytime? +
Yes. Cancel anytime from your profile — no phone calls, no hoops, no retention tricks. If you choose the annual plan ($350/year), you save over $100 compared to monthly. Both plans include full access to all 15 tools, progress tracking, and career intelligence features.
What if I just need help with one thing — like a contract review? +
Start the free trial. Run the contract review tool. If that is all you need, you will have your answer in 10 minutes. But most physicians who come for one tool discover gaps they did not know they had — financial planning they have been ignoring, a match competitiveness score lower than they expected, or a career roadmap they never built. The trial exists so you can find out.

The Journey

From medical student to Chief Resident, cardiology fellow, and practicing interventional cardiologist — every stage of my career required navigating high-stakes decisions about training, specialization, contracts, and long-term financial strategy. Most of the time, I was figuring it out alone.

I watched talented colleagues make career-defining mistakes — signing bad contracts, choosing specialties on gut feeling, entering the job market with no financial plan — because the frameworks didn't exist. The mentorship was scattered. The guidance was generic.

I built HeartWise so no physician has to navigate these decisions in the dark. Every tool is something I wish I'd had — or something I've seen a colleague desperately need.

"The decisions that shape your career shouldn't be made in the dark."
